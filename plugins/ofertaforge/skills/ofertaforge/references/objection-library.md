# OfertaForge — Objection Library
*Negotiator reference. Core rule: **trade, don't cave.** Every concession buys something. Never go below floor.*

## Concession ladder (give in this order, each only in exchange for something)
1. **Payment terms** — larger upfront % → small discount.
2. **Timeline** — a longer deadline → small discount (frees scheduling).
3. **Scope trim** — remove a deliverable → lower price (price follows scope).
4. **Bundle up** — add value instead of cutting price.
5. **Rights** — case-study / testimonial / referral → small discount.
6. **Phase it** — Phase 1 at reduced scope to start.
7. *Last resort* — a small price move within the Target→Floor band.
**Below floor → walk.**

## Common objections → response
- **"Too expensive."** Reframe to value/ROI; compare to the cost of the problem (or of a cheap redo); offer the Good tier or a scope cut — not a bare discount.
- **"Competitor is cheaper."** Differentiate (senior solo, multi-discipline, speed, reliability); ask what's in their quote; hold or trade.
- **"No budget right now."** Phase the work; smaller Phase 1; payment plan; reserve a slot.
- **"Send me your best price."** Don't collapse to floor; restate the Better-tier value; offer exactly one traded concession.
- **"Cheaper if it's quick/simple?"** Yes — by trimming scope. Anchor the trade explicitly.
- **"Let me think about it."** Agree a date; surface the real hesitation; offer to hold the quote/slot for X days.
- **"Why so much vs Fiverr / cheap freelancers?"** Quality, reliability, ownership, revisions, communication, no costly rework.
- **"Can you match this number?"** Only if it's ≥ floor and you trade for it; otherwise hold and justify.

## Anchoring
Open at the Anchor / Best number. Make the first figure the high one, then let the client move you toward Target. Protect Floor silently.

## Walk line
If the client needs below floor, decline warmly, leave the door open, and offer a referral. A clean walk protects brand and time — and often brings the client back.
