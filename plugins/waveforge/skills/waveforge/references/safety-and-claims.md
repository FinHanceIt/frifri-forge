# Safety & honest claims — WaveForge shared brain

This is the line WaveForge never crosses. Binaural beats are a **wellness / relaxation aid, not a medical treatment**. The Mastering & QC agent enforces the safety gate; every agent that writes listener-facing text enforces the claims rules.

## What the evidence actually says (stay honest)
- Several systematic reviews and meta-analyses report **small-to-moderate** effects on **anxiety, attention/memory, and sleep quality** — but results are **mixed**: roughly a third of studies find no significant effect, sample sizes are small, and designs are heterogeneous.
- Honest summary to lean on: *"Research is emerging and mixed; many listeners find these tracks help them relax, focus, or wind down, and effects vary from person to person."*
- Never present binaural beats as settled science or as a cure.

## Contraindications — surface the relevant warning
- **Epilepsy / seizure history:** consult a doctor before use, *especially* low-frequency (<10 Hz) and strongly pulsed/isochronic tracks.
- **Pacemaker / heart-rhythm conditions:** use with caution.
- **Pregnancy:** keep it gentle, make no claims.
- **Children:** evidence is weak; never use medical framing, keep volume low, and treat it as ambient sound — not a sleep treatment.
- **Auditory sensitivity** (tinnitus, hyperacusis) **or anxiety disorders:** may find it uncomfortable; start very quiet, offer to stop.

## Usage safety (include the relevant points in every release)
- **Headphones required** for binaural tracks (offer an isochronic version for speakers).
- Keep **volume moderate (≤ ~60%)**; protect hearing on long sessions; take breaks.
- **Never listen while driving or operating machinery** — delta/theta tracks induce drowsiness.
- Stop and rest if you feel dizzy, anxious, or unwell.

## Honest-claims rules
**ALLOWED** (benefit-framed, hedged): "may help you relax / focus / fall asleep", "designed to support a calm state", "many listeners use this to wind down", "for relaxation and focus".

**BANNED** (never write these): "cures", "treats [any condition]", "clinically proven to heal", "guaranteed results", "replaces medication/therapy", "boosts IQ", "detoxes / releases toxins", "heals chakras/cells", "cures insomnia/anxiety/depression", any specific medical or diagnostic outcome, any dosage-style medical instruction.

Rule: every benefit claim is **hedged** ("may", "designed to", "many find") and paired with **"results vary"** plus the disclaimer below.

## Disclaimer templates (attach to every published track)
**EN:** "For relaxation and wellbeing only. This is not medical advice or treatment, and results vary. Use headphones and keep the volume moderate. Do not listen while driving or operating machinery. If you have epilepsy, a heart condition, or are pregnant, talk to a doctor first. Stop if you feel unwell."

**RO:** "Doar pentru relaxare și stare de bine. Nu este sfat sau tratament medical, iar rezultatele diferă de la o persoană la alta. Folosește căști și ține volumul moderat. Nu asculta în timp ce conduci sau operezi utilaje. Dacă ai epilepsie, o afecțiune cardiacă sau ești însărcinată, discută mai întâi cu un medic. Oprește dacă te simți rău."

## Safety gate checklist (Mastering & QC must pass all before release)
1. If target beat < 10 Hz or content is isochronic/pulsed → epilepsy warning present.
2. Headphone requirement stated (binaural) or isochronic alternative provided.
3. Volume + "no driving/machinery" guidance present.
4. Listener-facing copy contains **zero banned claims**, all benefits hedged.
5. Correct RO/EN disclaimer attached.
6. True-peak ≤ −1 dBTP and loudness within target (no dangerously loud master).
