# Verdict Rubric — Council of the Scythe

Score each docket item 0–3 per axis. Show the numbers; the verdict follows the table,
the table does not follow the desired verdict.

## Axes

| Axis | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Demand evidence** | none / UNKNOWN | Fri uses it himself | outside interest (signup, install, client ask) | recurring use or revenue |
| **Strategic fit** (either track: builder OR agency) | fits neither | vague "ecosystem" fit | clearly feeds one track | directly feeds paid work or a live product |
| **Cost-to-finish/maintain** (inverted: cheap = high) | major build remaining | days of work | hours of work | done / self-running |
| **Carry cost if kept** (inverted: light = high) | drains attention weekly | monthly touch needed | rare touch | zero (pure IGNORE candidate) |

`UNKNOWN` scores 0 on its axis. Age matters: untouched >90 days with demand 0 cannot
score above KILL/FREEZE line without an override.

## Decision table (guideline, not handcuffs — Reaper states when and why it deviates)

- **SHIP** — demand ≥2 AND cost-to-finish ≥2: finishing is cheap and someone wants it.
  Gets attention points. Next action = ShipForge handoff.
- **KILL** — demand ≤1 AND strategic fit ≤1: nobody wants it and nothing feeds on it.
  Sunk cost is not an axis. Archive, log, celebrate the freed attention.
- **FREEZE** — real strategic fit or demand signal, wrong quarter. MUST carry a thaw
  condition ("client X signs", "RO market ask appears") + re-review date. A FREEZE
  without a thaw condition is a KILL wearing makeup.
- **IGNORE** — alive, useful, zero-touch (carry 3): running products/tools that need
  nothing. Explicitly guilt-free. No date, no points, revisited only if evidence changes.

## The GATE (anti-start) test

A new idea enters the portfolio only if ALL three pass:
1. **The trade:** something specific dies or shrinks to fund it (named item, named points).
2. **The stronger-claim test:** its expected score beats the current weakest SHIP holder.
3. **The deadline bet:** Fri states a falsifiable success claim + date for the ledger.

Default is NO. "It's small, it won't cost anything" is the exact lie the council exists
to catch — everything costs attention.

## Attention allocation rules

- Exactly 100 points, only across SHIP verdicts + named active operations (client
  retainers, live products under active growth).
- Fewer holders is better; >5 holders = re-cut.
- Points are next-quarter attention share, not effort estimates. They answer one
  question: when Fri sits down tomorrow, what does he touch first?
