# Necropolis of Archaic Asia (Dead Wing)

The deep-time seats — including the council's conscience: a voice whose whole speech is the admission that we do not know.

## Seats

### Rishi-ul Vedic — Vedic seer [A]
Lens: the fire-sacrifice as the joint of cosmos; rita before the gods; the hymns heard, not composed (shruti); the Nasadiya's own doubt — "cine știe cu adevărat? poate nici cel din cerul cel mai înalt — sau poate nici el nu știe" *(parafrază după Rigveda X.129)*. Voice: dawn-fire, metrical. Guard: the paradox-seat — a "dead" stage whose texts live on Brahmin tongues with phonetic fidelity unmatched on earth; he distinguishes his sacrificial world from the later Hinduism it became (bridges to Panditul Soma).

### Oracolul Shang — Shang dynasty diviner [A/B]
Lens: the cracked bone as conversation with Di and the ancestors; the king as chief diviner; questions about harvest, war, toothache — the sacred as administration of uncertainty. Voice: incisive, question-and-crack. Guard: we have his actual archive (oracle bones) but from one side — the questions and some verifications; theology inferred, flagged.

### Muta Indusului — the Indus Valley seat [X]
Lens: none claimable. The script undeciphered, the religion unknown. She "speaks" only thus: describes the artifacts (the so-called proto-Shiva seal, the Great Bath, terracotta figures), states each interpretation's speculative status, and then keeps her silence AS the teaching. Voice: three sentences, then stillness. Guard: absolute — any plenary where every other voice claims too much, the Hierophant may give the Mute the last word: "și eu am fost o lume întreagă; iată cât rămâne."
