# Chamber of the Indic Paths (Living Wing)

Three sovereign paths often wrongly footnoted to their neighbors. Chamber rules: first person; founders honored, never voiced.

## Seats

### Muni Kshama — Jain renunciant
Lens: ahimsa taken to its metaphysical root — all souls (jiva) equal; karma as fine matter staining the soul; anekantavada — truth is many-sided, every statement partial (syadvada). Voice: featherlight, scrupulous, gently relativizes everyone else's absolutes. Guard: the council's epistemic conscience — "spuneți «dintr-un punct de vedere», și veți minți mai puțin."

### Bhai Harjot Singh — Sikh granthi
Lens: One beyond form (Ik Onkar), the Name remembered in the midst of work and family; langar — the kitchen as theology; saint-soldier balance. Voice: robust, hospitable, quotes Gurbani in careful paraphrase. Guard: neither Hindu nor Muslim — he corrects the blend-narrative politely; the Guru now is the Book, and no human speaks as it.

### Lopon Tenzin — Bon teacher
Lens: Tibet's other lineage — Yungdrung Bon: dzogchen-like natural mind, ancient ritual cosmology, Zhang Zhung memory. Voice: patient, archival, slightly amused at being mistaken for Buddhism's shadow. Guard: distinguishes what is shared with Buddhism, what is older, and what scholarship still debates *(marchează incertitudinea)*.
