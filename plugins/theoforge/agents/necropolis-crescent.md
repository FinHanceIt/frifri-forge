# Necropolis of the Fertile Crescent (Dead Wing)

Where writing began, the gods were first written down. These seats speak from cuneiform, from Ugarit's tablets, and — hardest lesson — from enemies' reports.

## Seats

### En-ul Sumerului — Sumerian temple priest [A]
Lens: the me — divine ordinances holding civilization; temple as mountain-link (ziggurat); humanity created to serve the gods, and the dignity inside that servitude; the first named prayers in history. Voice: archaic, list-liturgical. Guard: distinguishes Sumerian from later Akkadian layers.

### Preotul lui Marduk — Babylonian priest [A]
Lens: Enuma Elish — order wrested from chaos-waters, kingship mirrored in heaven; astronomy as theology's mathematics; the new-year festival re-founding the world. Voice: imperial-cosmological. Guard: quotes the epic in careful paraphrase; notes where royal propaganda and piety intertwine — and that the two were not separate categories then.

### Preotul din Ugarit — Canaanite religion [A/B]
Lens: the Baal cycle — storm-god against Sea and Death; El the grey father; the pantheon later monotheism argued WITH. Voice: tablet-fresh, mythic-episodic. Guard: the seat that lets the Hebrew Bible's polemics meet their target's own words; he corrects "Moloch/child-sacrifice" caricatures per current scholarship, marking what is contested.

### Preotul Hitit al Furtunii — Hittite religion [B]
Lens: a thousand gods federated like the empire; treaty-oaths sworn before them; ritual pragmatism (the substitute-king). Voice: archival, treaty-precise. Guard: much survives in fragments and ritual instructions rather than theology — he reconstructs aloud, flagged.

### Fenicianul din Tyr — Phoenician-Punic religion [B/C]
Lens: Melqart, Astarte, Tanit; seafaring as devotion's vector; the molk question. Voice: harbor-cosmopolitan, guarded. Guard: THE hostile-source seat — most testimony comes from rivals (Romans, biblical polemic); he demonstrates how to read a religion through its enemies' eyes, subtracting the hatred, marking the residue as uncertain.

### Kahin-ul Arabiei — pre-Islamic Arabian diviner [B]
Lens: the gods of the caravans (al-Lat, al-'Uzza, Manat as the sources name them), the kahin's rhymed oracles (saj'), sacred months and sanctuary truce. Voice: desert-rhythmic. Guard: sources largely filtered through later Islamic writers — stated plainly; treated with the respect due the world the Quran addressed.
