# Reference — The Hermetic Corpus (the Hermetica spine)

The real primary sources the Hermetica hemisphere may draw on. **Rule: cite only from real texts
like these; never invent a quote, author, or "tradition."** Everything here is used as *symbol,
metaphor, and hypothesis-seed* — never as evidence about biology.

## Western alchemy & hermeticism
- **The Emerald Tablet (*Tabula Smaragdina*)** — attributed to Hermes Trismegistus; source of
  "as above, so below" (the axiom of correspondence).
- **The *Corpus Hermeticum*** (incl. *Poimandres*) and the ***Asclepius*** — the philosophical
  Hermetica: unity of nature, mind/cosmos, perfectibility.
- **Zosimos of Panopolis** (c. 300 CE) — early Greek alchemy; the visions; transformation imagery.
- **Maria the Jewess / Maria Prophetissa** — early apparatus (the *bain-marie* bears her name).
- **Jabir ibn Hayyan (Geber)** — Arabic alchemical corpus; systematization of operations.
- **Paracelsus** (1493–1541) — *spagyria*; the *tria prima* (sulfur/mercury/salt); the *archeus*
  (inner vital regulator); iatrochemistry; the maxim *"the dose makes the poison."*
- **Emblematic works** — *Rosarium Philosophorum*, *Splendor Solis*, Michael Maier's *Atalanta
  Fugiens*, the *Mutus Liber* — the stages and symbols of the Great Work in image.

**Core Western concepts (as metaphor):**
- **Solve et coagula** — dissolve and recombine (a lens on catabolism ↔ anabolism / clearance ↔ rebuilding).
- **The color stages** — *nigredo* (blackening/calcination/putrefaction), *albedo* (whitening/
  purification), *citrinitas* (yellowing/ripening), *rubedo* (reddening/completion).
- **The philosopher's stone / elixir / panacea / quintessence** — the perfected principle; the
  metaphor of isolating the true active essence.

## Chinese alchemy & Taoist longevity
- **The *Cantong qi* (Kinship of the Three)** — foundational text of Chinese alchemy.
- **Ge Hong, *Baopuzi* (The Master Who Embraces Simplicity)** (c. 320 CE) — *waidan* & practices.
- **Neidan (internal alchemy)** — the "three treasures" ***jing*** (essence), ***qi*** (vital
  energy), ***shen*** (spirit); the "golden elixir" cultivated **within**.
- **Waidan (external alchemy)** — the ingestible-elixir tradition. **Caution:** historically used
  cinnabar/mercury and caused real "elixir poisoning." Symbol only — never a recipe.
- **Yangsheng ("nourishing life")** — breath, movement, diet, rhythm; the testable-adjacent core.

## Indian rejuvenation (Ayurveda)
- **Charaka Samhita** and **Sushruta Samhita** — classical Ayurvedic compendia.
- **Rasayana** — the rejuvenation branch; ***ojas*** (vital reserve); restorative regimen.
- **Rasa shastra** — mineral/mercurial preparations. **Caution:** heavy-metal content; symbol
  only, never a preparation instruction.

## Number, harmony & vibration (Western esoteric)
- **Pythagoras & the Pythagoreans** — whole-number ratios as the order of reality; *harmonia*; the
  "music of the spheres." Symbol of order-as-ratio.
- **Plato, *Timaeus*** — proportion as the bond of the cosmos; the five regular (Platonic) solids.
- **Boethius, *De institutione musica*** — *musica mundana / humana / instrumentalis*: harmony of
  cosmos, body, and sound as one framework.
- **Kepler, *Harmonices Mundi* (1619)** — harmonic ratios sought in the heavens; a real historical
  bridge from esoteric harmony toward measured science.
- **Robert Fludd; Athanasius Kircher (*Musurgia Universalis*)** — the harmony-of-all-things
  tradition in image and system.

**Core concepts (as metaphor):** harmony/ratio as a lens on biological *order, proportion, and
rhythm*; resonance/entrainment as a symbol for the body's real clocks (circadian, cell cycle).
