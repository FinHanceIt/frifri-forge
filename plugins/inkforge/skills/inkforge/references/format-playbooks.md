# Format playbooks — structure per format, and how to break the tidy shape

`story-architect` uses these to build a **Structure Map**. Every playbook has a spine AND a
"break the AI shape" note (tell #5: over-orderly structure). Structure should serve the
piece, then get slightly, deliberately uneven.

## Fiction & stories
- Spine: a character wants something; something's in the way; scene by scene the pressure
  changes them. Enter each scene late, leave early. Show in scene, compress with summary.
- Break the shape: resist the neat moral and the bow-tie ending. Let a thread dangle. Trust
  subtext. A story can end on an image.

## Scripts & screenplays
- Spine: industry format (sluglines, action lines, character cue + dialogue). Beats: a loose
  3-act or Save-the-Cat skeleton; each scene turns on a value change. Dialogue carries
  subtext; people interrupt, dodge, don't answer.
- Break the shape: no on-the-nose lines, no "as you know" exposition. White space is
  pacing. Let a scene start mid-argument. Action lines get a voice too.

## Essays & articles
- Spine: a real thesis with a stake (something could be wrong). Lead with a specific — a
  scene, a number, an anecdote — not a definition. Argue, don't summarize; earn each turn.
- Break the shape: kill the five-paragraph skeleton and the "three balanced points". Let one
  point run long. Allow a digression that pays off. End on an implication or image, never a
  "In conclusion" restate.

## Long-form / books
- Spine: a throughline question; a chapter map where each chapter escalates or complicates;
  recurring motifs. Track what the reader knows and feels per chapter.
- Break the shape: vary chapter length and rhythm. A short, strange chapter between two long
  ones. Don't resolve everything on schedule.

## Marketing & copy
- Spine: one reader, one job, one promise. AIDA or PAS (problem-agitate-solve) as scaffolding
  — attention, then the specific, then the proof, then one clear ask.
- Break the shape: specificity sells better than adjectives ("ships in 2 days", not
  "fast"). Voice beats polish. One idea per asset. Cut the second CTA.
- Honesty: no fake scarcity, no invented testimonials, no fabricated stats.

## Academic-style
- Spine: a genuine argument/claim, evidence, a logical structure (context → problem →
  approach → evidence → discussion). Signpost with plain transitions, not "Moreover".
- Break the shape: human academic prose still has a voice and a stake; it isn't a wall of
  hedges and connectives. Vary sentence length even here.
- Honesty (hard): cite **real** sources only — never fabricate references, data, or quotes.
  This mode writes original argument and the author's own research/synthesis; it is **not**
  for producing graded work to be passed off as someone else's (see `integrity-boundary.md`).
