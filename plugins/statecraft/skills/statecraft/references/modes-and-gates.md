# Statecraft — Modes, Quorum & Gates

How each mode runs, who must be seated (quorum), and where the human/hard gates sit. The
Cabinet Secretary owns sequencing.

## Quorum rule (the checks must speak)
A **major decision** (new law, budget, war/peace, rights, anything moving public-trust or
unrest more than a little) may not be delivered until the checks have spoken:
legality (`the-bench`), opposition (`the-opposition`), and public reaction
(`the-citizenry`, and `the-press` where information matters). A **minor decision** (routine
admin) may skip to `the-mandarin` + the one owning ministry + `the-registrar`.
`the-tribunal` verifies the quorum was met before delivery.

## FOUND — constitute a state
`the-charter` (regime, matrix, franchise, anchor) → `the-registrar` seeds the National
Ledger (vitals, factions, population). → **[Charter gate: Fri confirms]**. Nothing governs
until the charter is confirmed.

## GOVERN — a problem → a decided policy
1. `the-mandarin`: frame the real question, 2–4 options, each costed against the ledger.
2. Seat the 2–4 relevant ministries; they argue their portfolios; reconcile contradictions.
3. Checks: `the-bench` (is it constitutional?), `the-opposition` (the counter-case),
   `the-citizenry` + `the-press` (how the public reacts).
4. `the-premier` (and `the-sovereign` where assent/veto applies): the decision, with the
   trade-off named and dissent recorded.
5. **[Enactment gate: Fri approves the pen]** → `the-registrar` logs the ledger delta.
6. `the-tribunal` review → deliver.

## LEGISLATE — bill → law
draft (`the-chancery-of-justice`, model legislative text) → cost (`the-mandarin`) →
`the-assembly` debate + amendments + vote (model the majority/committee reality) →
`the-opposition` on the record → `the-sovereign` assent → `the-bench` constitutional
review (can strike) → **[Enactment gate]** → `the-registrar` enacts + adds to ACTIVE LAWS.

## CRISIS — a shock hits now
`the-warden-of-crises` convenes the war-room and states the clock → security/relevant
ministries (`the-marshal`, `the-cipher`, `the-interior`, `the-treasury`, `the-envoy` as
fits) give fast options → `the-premier` decides under pressure → `the-citizenry`/`the-press`
reaction → `the-registrar` logs the shock and the response and any lingering damage.
Compressed, but the legality and public-cost checks are still named.

## TICK — advance a period
`the-registrar` advances the turn counter, fires due PENDING EVENTS (seeded by
`the-oracle`), updates vitals in bands with causes, and surfaces the 1–3 decisions Fri now
faces. Preview needs no human gate; acting on a surfaced decision routes to GOVERN/CRISIS.

## AUDIT — state of the nation
`the-registrar` (current vitals + trajectory) + `the-auditor` (integrity, corruption,
promise-vs-delivery) + `the-oracle` (where this is heading, scenarios) + `the-press` (how
it reads publicly) → one honest state-of-the-nation.

## BRIEF — one seat, one question
Dispatch a single agent. Label "brief — no gates run". Still passes `the-tribunal` if it
touches the safety boundary.

## Gates (summary)
- **Charter gate** (human) — confirm regime/constitution before governing.
- **Enactment gate** (human) — approve before writing to the National Ledger.
- **Review gate** (hard, `the-tribunal`) — never-fabricate, quorum met, boundary honored,
  charter-coherent. `PASS / PASS-WITH-FLAGS / BLOCK`.

## Cabinet seats (name them when routing GOVERN/CRISIS)
- Fiscal/economic: `the-treasury` · `the-mint` (independent) · `the-economy`.
- Internal/external: `the-interior` · `the-envoy` · `the-marshal` · `the-cipher`.
- Social/physical/justice: `the-commons` · `the-works` · `the-chancery-of-justice`.
Seat the 2–4 whose portfolio the matter touches; add the mandatory checks for major decisions.
